User Guide

How to create a customer-ready quote in HubSpot.

Overview: how quoting works

Creating a quote is simple. You enter the commercial details once on the Deal, label the Contacts who should appear on the quote, sync the roster, and HubSpot builds the customer-ready quote for you. You do not type anything into the quote template itself.

The five steps

1
Open the card. On the Deal, open the H2K Quote Details card.
2
Enter the details. Choose the quote type, complete the fields, then save.
3
Label and sync the people. Add the right association labels to the Deal's Contacts, then click Sync from Deal contacts.
4
Check it is ready. Make sure validation shows no errors.
5
Create the quote. Build the quote, review the preview, and send.

The simple idea

Label the Contacts on the Deal, then sync. The quote fills itself in from the labeled Contacts and the details you save. You never edit the quote template.

1. Enter the quote details

Open the Deal and open the H2K Quote Details card. Pick the quote type at the top, fill in the fields for that type, then click Save quote details.

If it is a Bootcamp quote

FieldWhat to enter
BootcampChoose the Bootcamp being sold.
Number of ParticipantsEnter how many seats you are selling.
Payment TypeChoose how the customer is paying.

If it is a CXO Membership quote

FieldWhat to enter
Membership TypeChoose Company, Individual, or Team. Required for CXO Membership quotes.
Number of MembersEnter how many memberships you are selling.
Membership Start DatePick the start date.
Membership CycleChoose the cycle, such as Annual.
Payment TypeChoose how the customer is paying.

Always save

Click Save quote details after any change. If you change something later, start a fresh quote draft so it picks up the latest details.

2. Sync the people from Deal contacts

The roster is the list of named people who appear on the quote. The roster now comes from the Deal's associated Contacts and their association labels.

To update the roster

  1. Open the Deal's associated Contacts.
  2. Confirm each Contact has the correct association label.
  3. Return to the H2K Quote Details card.
  4. Click Sync from Deal contacts.
  5. Review the roster table.

The roster table shows whether each row is Synced or Manual.

SourceWhat it means
SyncedThe row came from a labeled Contact association on the Deal. These rows are read-only in the card.
ManualThe row was added manually as a fallback. Manual entry is available only through the Manual entry fallback control.

Which labels create which rows

Deal-to-Contact labelRoster roleAppears on
Growth Room MemberMemberCXO Membership quote
CEO Boardroom MemberMemberCXO Membership quote
Bootcamp ParticipantParticipantBootcamp quote
Billing ContactBilling ContactBoth Bootcamp and CXO Membership quotes

A Contact can have more than one label

For example, if the same Contact is both a Member and the Billing Contact, the sync can create both roster rows.

Add or change someone

For normal quoting, update the Contact labels on the Deal and click Sync from Deal contacts.

Use manual entry only as a fallback when an admin or Indigo Trigger instructs you to do so. Manual rows can be edited from the card. Synced rows should be changed by updating the Contact or the Contact's Deal association labels, then syncing again.

Always include a Billing Contact

Every quote needs a Billing Contact. Label at least one Deal Contact as Billing Contact, then click Sync from Deal contacts. Without it, the quote is not ready to send.

Adding many people

For normal quoting, add the people as Contacts on the Deal and apply the correct association labels. Then click Sync from Deal contacts.

Bulk manual entry is now a fallback tool. Use it only when the people cannot be represented as labeled Deal Contacts.

Include on quote

Synced rows are included automatically and are read-only in the card. To remove a synced person from the quote, remove or change the Contact's association label on the Deal, then click Sync from Deal contacts again.

Manual fallback rows can still be edited manually.

3. Check it is ready

The Validation section tells you whether the quote is ready. Clear any errors before you create the quote. Warnings are reminders to double-check something, and do not block you.

What you seeWhat to do
No issues foundYou are ready to create the quote.
No billing contact included (error)Label a Deal Contact as Billing Contact, then click Sync from Deal contacts.
Membership type is required for a CXO Membership quote (error)Choose Company, Individual, or Team in the Membership type field, then save.
Count does not match (warning)Check whether the number sold and the named people should match, and fix whichever is wrong. It is fine to sell more seats than the names you have so far.

When the details and people are ready, create the quote the normal HubSpot way. The quote fills itself in from the Deal.

  2. Choose the template that matches the quote type.
  3. Review the quote name, expiration date, and terms.

5. What appears on each quote

A Bootcamp quote shows

  • The Bootcamp details you entered.
  • Your included Participants.
  • Your included Billing Contact.
  • Products, totals, and terms.

A CXO Membership quote shows

  • The membership details you entered.
  • Your included Members.
  • Your included Billing Contact.
  • Products, totals, and terms.

Each quote shows only its own people

A Bootcamp quote does not show Members, and a Membership quote does not show Participants. That happens automatically.

Before you send

Quick check

  • For CXO quotes, Membership type is selected.
  • The Deal Contacts have the correct labels.
  • The roster has been synced from Deal contacts.
  • A Billing Contact is included.
  • Validation shows no errors.
  • The preview looks right.

H2K Revenue Room

Operations Guide

The complete reference for creating and administering H2K quotes.

Overview

Enter the information once on the Deal, then let the quote template render it automatically. Quote creators should not manually re-enter membership, participant, member, or billing information inside template modules.

The five-step workflow

1
Prepare the Deal. Confirm the Company, Contact, line items, and quote type are correct.
2
Complete Quote Details. Use the H2K Quote Details card to save the Bootcamp or CXO commercial fields.
3
Sync the roster. Label the Deal's Contacts, then click Sync from Deal contacts to build the roster.
4
Clear validation. Resolve blocking issues and review any soft count warnings.
5
Create and review the quote. Choose the correct automated v2 template, inspect the preview, then save and send.

What the system uses as the source of truth

InformationWhere it comes fromWhat it controls
Products and pricingHubSpot line itemsProducts and Services, quantity, price, subtotal, and total
Bootcamp or CXO commercial detailsDeal fields saved through the H2K Quote Details cardBootcamp and Membership Details sections
Membership TypeStandard Deal property membership_typeCXO Membership Details section
Participant, Member, and Billing peopleDeal Contacts with association labels, synced into roster rowsParticipant, Member, and Billing Contact tables
Quote terms and deliveryHubSpot quote wizardExpiration date, comments, purchase terms, signature, payment, and delivery

Critical operating rule

The commercial quantity and the named roster are separate. Number of Participants or Number of Members is the quoted quantity. The roster is the list of named people.

The system warns when the two do not match, but it does not automatically overwrite either one.

1. Enter and save quote details

Open the Deal and use the H2K Quote Details card. Select the correct quote type, complete the fields shown for that type, then click Save quote details before creating the quote.

Revenue Room Bootcamp fields

FieldHow to use it
BootcampSelect the Bootcamp offering that should appear in the quote.
Number of ParticipantsEnter the commercial quantity being quoted. Do not calculate this from the named roster.
Payment TypeSelect the agreed payment method for the Bootcamp engagement.

Revenue Room CXO Membership fields

FieldHow to use it
Membership TypeSelect the membership offering that should appear in the quote.
Number of MembersEnter the commercial quantity being quoted.
Membership Start DateSelect the start date. The automated quote formats it as a customer-friendly date.
Membership CycleSelect the applicable cycle, such as Annual.
Payment TypeSelect the agreed membership payment method.

Save before quoting

After any change to the quote details, click Save quote details. When you need to confirm updated data in a quote, create a brand-new draft rather than relying on an older draft preview.

What the card does automatically

  • Shows only the fields that belong to the selected quote type.
  • Saves the commercial data on the Deal.
  • Reloads the roster after successful changes.
  • Validates required roster coverage and quantity mismatches.
  • Feeds the automated Bootcamp or CXO quote template.

2. Sync and maintain the deal roster

The roster is the named-person layer of the quote. For normal quoting, the roster is generated from labeled Deal-to-Contact associations.

The sync reads the Deal's Contacts and creates read-only roster rows from these labels:

LabelRoster role
Growth Room MemberMember
CEO Boardroom MemberMember
Bootcamp ParticipantParticipant
Billing ContactBilling Contact

Click Sync from Deal contacts after changing Contact labels.

Synced and manual rows

Row sourceHow to maintain it
SyncedUpdate the Contact or the Contact's association label on the Deal, then sync again. Synced rows are read-only in the card.
ManualUse the Manual entry fallback controls in the card.

Manual rows are preserved

Syncing from Deal contacts does not delete manual rows. Manual entry is a fallback, used only when people cannot be represented as labeled Deal Contacts.

Sync behavior

  • The sync is manual and idempotent. Running it again with no Contact-label changes does not create duplicates.
  • If a labeled Contact changes roles, the synced roster rows update to match the Deal's labels.
  • To remove a synced person from the quote, remove or change the Contact's association label on the Deal, then sync again.

Every quote needs a Billing Contact

Label at least one Deal Contact as Billing Contact, then click Sync from Deal contacts. Without an included Billing Contact, the quote does not pass validation.

3. Bulk add roster rows (fallback)

For normal quoting, label Deal Contacts and use Sync from Deal contacts, described in Section 2. Bulk add is a manual fallback. Use it only when people cannot be represented as labeled Deal Contacts. When you do use it, the workflow is paste, parse, preview, correct, and confirm, and it creates manual rows that the sync preserves.

Choose how roles should be assigned

ModeUse it whenRole behavior
One role for every rowEveryone in the pasted list has the same role.Choose Participant, Member, or Billing Contact once. That role applies to every row.
Use the Role column from the pasted dataThe pasted list mixes Participants, Members, and Billing Contacts.Each row must contain a valid role or be corrected in the preview.

Preview behavior

Preview statusMeaningCan it be saved?
ReadyThe row passed parsing and validation.Yes
WarningThe row can save, but review issues such as an invalid-looking email or a likely duplicate.Yes
BlockedA required value is missing or invalid, such as Full name or a valid role in mixed-role mode.No

FailedThe create attempt failed. Correct or retry only the unsaved row.Retry

Duplicates are warnings, not automatic deletions

The system may warn when a pasted row looks like another pasted row or an existing roster row. Review the row and decide whether the duplicate is intentional.

4. Use validation as the pre-quote quality gate

The Validation section checks the commercial details and the current deal roster. Resolve errors before creating the quote. Warnings should be reviewed, but they do not automatically block the quote.

Validation messageTypeWhat it meansWhat to do
No billing contact is included on the quoteErrorThere is no included Billing Contact row.Label a Deal Contact as Billing Contact, then click Sync from Deal contacts.
Membership type is required for a CXO Membership quoteErrorCXO Membership requires the standard Membership Type field.Choose Company, Individual, or Team and save the quote details.
Number of participants does not match included participant rowsWarningThe quoted Bootcamp quantity differs from the named included Participant count.Confirm whether the commercial quantity or the roster should be adjusted. The system will not overwrite either.
Number of members does not match included member rowsWarningThe quoted CXO quantity differs from the named included Member count.Confirm whether the commercial quantity or the roster should be adjusted.
No issues foundPassCurrent quote details and roster pass the card checks.Proceed to create the quote and verify the final preview.

Validation is template-aware

For Bootcamp, the count check uses included Participant rows. For CXO Membership, it uses included Member rows. Billing validation uses included Billing Contact rows for both templates.

5. Create and review the HubSpot quote

After Quote Details and roster validation are complete, use the standard HubSpot quote flow. The automated template reads the saved Deal data and roster when the quote is created.

Active automated templates

Quote typeSelect this template
Revenue Room Bootcamp[Template] Revenue Room Bootcamp v2
Revenue Room CXO Membership[Template] Revenue Room CXO Membership v2

Step by step

  1. Confirm the Deal has the correct Company, Contact, and line items.
  2. Start a new quote from the Deal.
  3. Review Buyer Info, Your Info, Line Items, and Signature and Payment.
  4. On Template and Details, choose the correct automated v2 template.
  5. Enter or review the quote name, domain, expiration date, language, comments, and purchase terms.
  6. Inspect the Quote preview. Verify the commercial section and the roster tables, not just the totals.
  7. Click Save or Next and complete the Review step.
  8. Send only after the final preview matches the Deal and roster.

Create a new draft after meaningful changes

When Quote Details, roster rows, or template logic has changed, create a brand-new draft quote for verification. An older draft may not reflect the newest rendered data.

7. Before-send review

Before generating a quote

  • The Deal uses the correct quote type.
  • All commercial fields in the H2K Quote Details card are complete and saved.
  • For CXO quotes, Membership Type is selected.
  • Line items, quantity, price, and billing terms are correct.
  • The Deal Contacts have the correct association labels.
  • A Deal Contact is labeled Billing Contact.
  • The roster has been synced from Deal contacts.
  • Validation shows no unresolved errors.

Before sending a quote

  • The correct automated v2 template is selected.
  • Buyer and seller information are correct.
  • Products, quantity, price, subtotal, total, and total contract value are correct.
  • Comments and purchase terms are accurate.
  • The Bootcamp or Membership Details section matches the Deal.
  • Only the intended Participants or Members appear.
  • The intended Billing Contact appears.
  • People without a quote label do not appear.
  • The expiration date, signature, and payment settings are correct.

When to escalate

Escalate when the saved Deal and roster are correct, a new draft uses the correct automated template, and the rendered quote is still wrong. Include the Deal link, template name, screenshot, expected result, and actual result.
